# Certified Writing Room

## Purpose
This document gives an informative description of a physical environment that may support higher-assurance OHAS workflows.

## Why a Physical Space Matters
Digital logs alone cannot eliminate all risks. A controlled writing room can improve custody, supervision, and trust, especially for D3 and D4 workflows.

## Possible Models

### Light Controlled Station
A single managed workstation with device restrictions and logged sessions.

### Supervised Room
A room with controlled entry, device storage, managed workstation, and documented session handling.

### High-Assurance Secure Writing Room
A stronger environment with restricted network access, documented custody procedures, and enhanced supervision.

## Typical Controls
- identity check
- session registration
- device deposit
- controlled workstation
- restricted or absent network access
- material handling procedure for handwritten notes
- session closure and evidence export

## Typical Outputs
A session may produce:

- session identifier
- workstation identifier
- room identifier
- timestamps
- output snapshot
- logs
- supporting custody record

## Relation to OHAS Levels
A physical writing room is optional at lower levels and increasingly relevant at higher levels. It is especially aligned with `D4`.

## Notes
Any implementation should also consider privacy, accessibility, safety, and local legal requirements.
