# OHAS Use Cases

## Purpose
This document describes representative ways OHAS may be applied.

## 1. Independent Publishing
An author wants to publish a book with a public statement that the certified text was written without generative AI.

A likely configuration:
- Profile: `P1`
- Assurance: `D1` or `D2`

## 2. Traditional Publishing
A publisher wants to certify the manuscript text while allowing AI use for marketing or cover ideation outside certified scope.

A likely configuration:
- Profile: `P2`
- Assurance: `D2` or `D3`

## 3. Transparent Hybrid Publishing
A publisher or author wants to disclose bounded AI use while maintaining a human-primary authorship claim.

A likely configuration:
- Profile: `P3`
- Assurance: `D1` to `D3`

## 4. Educational Assessment
A school or university wants a repeatable and defensible way to document supervised writing integrity.

A likely configuration:
- Profile: `P4`
- Assurance: `D2` or `D3`

## 5. Literary Prize or Competition
Organizers want a fairer and more evidence-based framework for human-only categories.

A likely configuration:
- Profile: `P5`
- Assurance: `D1` for entry, `D2` or `D3` for finalists

## 6. Prestige or Archival Workflow
A foundation or archive wants a high-assurance record for historically significant works.

A likely configuration:
- Profile: `P1` or `P2`
- Assurance: `D4`

## Notes
These examples are illustrative. OHAS is intentionally flexible so that adopters can document additional categories and local procedures.
