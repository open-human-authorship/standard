# FAQ

## Does OHAS prove with certainty that a book was written only by a human?
No. OHAS documents and evaluates a process. It can provide stronger or weaker assurance depending on the selected level, but it does not claim absolute proof.

## Is OHAS based on AI detectors?
No. OHAS is based primarily on declarations, provenance, version records, workflow evidence, and audit. AI detectors may be used, if at all, only as weak secondary signals.

## What is the difference between a profile and an assurance level?
A profile defines the policy model, such as no generative AI or educational strict mode. An assurance level defines how strong the evidence and controls are.

## Can OHAS be used when AI is allowed outside the text itself?
Yes. That is one reason profiles such as `P2` and `P3` exist.

## Is a certified writing room mandatory?
No. It is optional, but it becomes highly relevant for stronger levels such as `D4`.

## Can OHAS be used in schools or contests?
Yes. Profiles `P4` and `P5` are intended for those contexts.

## Is OHAS only for books?
No. It can also apply to essays, scripts, poems, theses, or other defined textual works.

## Can the standard evolve?
Yes. The repository is designed for open contribution and revision.
