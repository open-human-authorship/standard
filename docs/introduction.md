# Introduction to OHAS

## What OHAS Is
OHAS stands for **Open Human Authorship Standard**.

It is an open framework for documenting and evaluating claims that a text was created through a human-authored process without generative AI within the certified scope, or with clearly disclosed limits and assistance depending on the selected profile.

## What OHAS Is Not
OHAS is not a magical detector of whether a text is human. It does not claim to prove an absolute metaphysical absence of AI use.

Instead, OHAS focuses on:

- declared scope
- documented process
- evidence quality
- chain of custody
- assurance levels
- public transparency

## Why OHAS Exists
As generative tools become common, authors, educators, publishers, contest organizers, and readers increasingly need a more credible option than unsupported declarations or unreliable AI detectors.

OHAS provides a structured alternative based on provenance and verification.

## Core Components
OHAS combines several ideas:

- policy profiles
- assurance levels
- evidence bundles
- manifests and version records
- optional audit workflows
- optional certified writing rooms for stronger assurance

## Intended Users
OHAS may be useful for:

- independent authors
- publishers
- literary prizes
- educational institutions
- archives
- foundations
- researchers studying authorship workflows

## Repository Structure
The repository typically includes:

- the core standard
- profiles
- schemas
- examples
- governance files
- informative documents for audit and operational guidance

## Design Philosophy
OHAS prefers transparency over absolutism and documented process over speculative detection.
