# Revocation Process

## Status
Informative guidance aligned with `OHAS_STANDARD.md` v0.1.

## Purpose
This document outlines a practical process for suspension, challenge, and revocation of OHAS certifications.

## Triggers
A revocation review may be triggered by:

- evidence tampering concerns
- substantiated challenge from a third party
- discovery of undeclared prohibited tool use
- false or materially misleading declarations
- critical chain-of-custody failures
- issuer review of new facts

## Possible Outcomes
- no action
- clarification issued
- certification status updated
- temporary suspension
- downgrade of assurance level
- revocation

## Baseline Process

### 1. Intake of Challenge or Incident
The issuer records the trigger and opens a case file.

### 2. Preliminary Review
The issuer determines whether the matter is credible enough to proceed.

### 3. Evidence Preservation
Relevant logs, manifests, reports, and declarations should be preserved.

### 4. Notice
Where appropriate, the affected author or applicant should be informed and allowed to respond.

### 5. Review
A documented review is conducted, internally or by an independent party.

### 6. Decision
The issuer records the outcome and rationale.

### 7. Publication or Status Update
If the certification has public status, the public record should be updated accordingly.

## Public Status Suggestions
Public status labels may include:

- valid
- under review
- suspended
- revoked
- replaced
- downgraded

## Appeals
Issuers should define whether appeals are allowed and within what time period.

## Notes
Revocation mechanisms should be fair, evidence-based, and documented. OHAS should not support opaque or arbitrary enforcement.
