# P5 — Prize / Competition Mode

## Status
Draft profile for OHAS v0.1.

## Purpose
This profile is intended for literary prizes, writing competitions, fellowships, and juried submissions that require a high-confidence statement about human authorship under clearly published rules.

## Primary Goals
- Ensure fair competition conditions
- Increase trust in submissions and judging outcomes
- Support transparent, contest-specific authorship requirements

## Typical Contexts
- Literary contests
- Scriptwriting competitions
- Residency applications with original writing samples
- Shortlisted submissions requiring elevated scrutiny
- Special categories for human-only authorship

## Allowed
- Basic file saving and backup
- Formatting and layout tools
- Controlled citation use where relevant to the contest category
- Accessibility tools approved by contest policy

## Restricted / Contest-Defined
- Network access during monitored sessions
- Import of prior drafts
- Handwritten notes or planning materials
- External editorial assistance
- Grammar and proofreading tools

## Prohibited
- Any generative AI contribution to the certified competition text, unless the contest explicitly defines a separate category
- AI-assisted rewriting, paraphrasing, expansion, summarization, or translation of the certified text
- Ghostwriting or undisclosed third-party text assistance
- Unlogged import of substantial external material

## Competition Administration Expectations
P5 should normally include:
- published entrant rules
- formal submission declarations
- defined evidence requirements for finalists or winners
- a documented review and challenge procedure
- explicit sanctions for false declarations

## Required Disclosures
- category and rule set under which the submission is entered
- whether the certification applies to the submitted text only or to the entire drafting history
- any permitted editorial or accessibility accommodations
- any declared external assistance allowed by the competition

## Evidence Expectations
- entrant declaration
- final work hash
- version history or controlled-session evidence for shortlisted entries, finalists, or winners
- rule acknowledgment record
- audit trail where the contest requires elevated review

## Typical Assurance Pairings
- D0 or D1 for open-entry initial submissions
- D2 for shortlisted entries
- D3 or D4 for finalists, major awards, or challenged works

## Enforcement Considerations
Competition organizers should define:
- review triggers
- challenge windows
- appeal procedures
- remedies, including disqualification or revocation

## Notes
P5 is especially useful when contest organizers want a documented and reviewable standard instead of relying on informal accusations or unreliable AI detectors.
